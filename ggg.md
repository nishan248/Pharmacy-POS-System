In a pharmacy, the **purchasing process** and **Goods Received Note (GRN) process** are important parts of inventory management. A typical workflow is as follows:

## 1. Purchasing Process

### Step 1: Identify Need

* Check current stock levels.
* Identify medicines or medical supplies that need replenishment.
* Consider minimum stock levels, demand, and expiry dates.

### Step 2: Prepare Purchase Order (PO)

* Create a Purchase Order listing:

  * Item name
  * Strength and dosage form
  * Quantity required
  * Supplier details
  * Agreed price

### Step 3: Approval

* Pharmacist-in-charge or management reviews and approves the PO.
* Budget and supplier selection are verified.

### Step 4: Send Order to Supplier

* The approved PO is sent to the selected supplier or wholesaler.
* Supplier confirms availability and delivery date.

### Step 5: Receive Delivery

* Supplier delivers the ordered items along with an invoice or delivery note.

---

## 2. GRN (Goods Received Note) Process

A **Goods Received Note (GRN)** is a document prepared when goods are received from the supplier.

### Step 1: Verify Delivered Items

Check:

* Item names
* Strength and dosage forms
* Quantities received
* Batch numbers
* Expiry dates
* Physical condition of products

### Step 2: Compare with Purchase Order

Ensure the received goods match:

* Purchase Order (PO)
* Supplier invoice/delivery note

### Step 3: Record Discrepancies

If there are:

* Shortages
* Damaged items
* Wrong products
* Near-expiry products

These should be documented and reported to the supplier.

### Step 4: Prepare GRN

The GRN usually contains:

* GRN number
* Date received
* Supplier name
* Purchase Order number
* Item details
* Quantity ordered and received
* Batch number and expiry date
* Signature of receiving pharmacist/storekeeper

### Step 5: Update Inventory System

* Enter received quantities into the pharmacy inventory system.
* Stock balances are automatically updated.

### Step 6: Store Products

* Arrange products according to storage requirements.
* Follow **FEFO (First Expiry, First Out)** principles.
* Store temperature-sensitive items in refrigerators if required.

### Step 7: Invoice Processing and Payment

* Finance/accounts department matches:

  * Purchase Order
  * GRN
  * Supplier Invoice
* Payment is made according to agreed terms.

This process helps ensure that the pharmacy receives the correct medicines, maintains accurate inventory records, and complies with pharmaceutical quality and safety standards.
